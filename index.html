Hi! I'm Divya Ramesh.

I'm a PhD candidate in Computer Science and Engineering at the University of Michigan - Ann Arbor, where I work with Nikola Banovic on the design, applications and ethics of human-AI interaction systems. I use a mix of quantiative and qualitative approaches such as machine learning, reinforcement learning, crowdsourcing, experiments and interviews to answer the following types of questions:

  • How could we mitigate algorithmic harm on-demand work?
  • How could we design algorithmic interventions to improve worker-platform relationships in on-demand work?
  • How could we adopt community-centered approaches for designing AI-mediated platforms to enable the future-of-work?

These questions, motivated by my commitment to algorithmic justice, invariably delve into algorithmic accountability, bias, fairness and transparency. My work aims to inform the design of AI systems that enhance worker agency, well-being, their access to economic opportunities and equity

Apart from research, I'm also a passionate advocate for diversity, equity and inclusion at work. I co-founded the Tech + Society Reading Group in CSE, and serve on the board of ECSEL+ and DEI committees in Computer Science.

Before starting my PhD, I led the research and development efforts at CloudSight Inc. At Cloudsight, I architected the company's first human-AI interaction pipeline for analyzing visual content in real-time, powering many visual recognition apps including the award-winning accessibility app, TapTapSee.

You can find my CV here. Please feel free to reach out to me if you would like to know more about my work!
