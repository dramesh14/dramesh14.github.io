Hi! I'm Divya Ramesh.

I'm a PhD candidate in Computer Science and Engineering at the University of Michigan - Ann Arbor, where I work with Nikola Banovic in the Interactive Systems Lab. My research, at the intersection of Artificial Intelligence (AI), Human-Computer Interaction (HCI), and Science & Technology Studies (STS), explores the design of algorithmic systems in the on-demand economy.

I use mixed methods and apply a critical lens to answer the following types of questions:

  • How could we reduce algorithmic harm in the on-demand economy?
  • How could we design algorithmic interventions to improve worker-platform relationships in on-demand work?
  • How could we adopt community-centered approaches for designing AI-mediated platforms for the future-of-work?

These questions are motivated by my commitment to algorithmic justice. My work aims to create technologies for mediation of work that increase worker well-being, their access to economic opportunities and equity. I believe that designing intelligble AI systems that enhance, rather than diminsh worker agency, is key.

Before starting my PhD, I led the research and development efforts at CloudSight Inc., where I worked on human-centered AI for vision. TapTapSee, the popular accessibility app, was one of CloudSight's products. Please feel free to reach out to me if you would like to know more about my work!
