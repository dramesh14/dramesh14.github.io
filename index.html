Hi! I'm Divya Ramesh.

I'm a PhD candidate in Computer Science and Engineering at the University of Michigan - Ann Arbor. My research interests lie at the intersection of human-computer interaction, public interest technology, and responsible AI. Specifically, I examine the social, ethical, and design implications of AI in high-stake decision-making domains.

I use a mix of qualitative and quantitative methods to broadly answer the following questions in my dissertation: a) How might we characterize the strengths and limitations of Human-centered AI approaches to responsible AI innovation? b) How we might reimagine responsible AI frameworks to center the perspectives of marginalized communities?

I have published in top-HCI and AI venues such as ACM CHI, FAccT, DIS, TOCHI and AAMAS. I have been recognized with a Best Paper nomination and Pragnesh Jay Modi Best Student Paper award. I am a recipient of the prestigious 2023-24 Quad Fellowship, an initiative of the governments of Australia, India, Japan and the US. I have also contributed to public conversations via popular media outlets such as CNBC TV18 and Techcrunch which influenced policy outcomes for Google in 2023.

Before starting my PhD, I led the research and development efforts at CloudSight Inc. At Cloudsight, I architected the company's first human-AI interaction pipeline for analyzing visual content in real-time, powering many visual recognition apps including the award-winning accessibility app, TapTapSee.

Please feel free to reach out to me by email if you would like to hear more about my work! My pronouns are she/her.
